Magnolia

Magnolia is a large genus that contains over 200 flowering plant species. It is named after French botanist Pierre Magnol and, having evolved before bees appeared, the flowers were developed to encourage pollination by beetle.

Magnolia grandiflora, commonly know as the Southern magnolia or Bull bay, is a tree of the family Magnoliaceae to the southern United States, from coastal Virginia South to central Florida, and west to eastern Texas and Oklahoma. Reaching 27.5 m (90 ft) in height, it is a large striking evergreen tree with large dark green leaves and large white frangrant flowers.Widely cultivated around the world, over a hundred cultivars have been bred and marketed commercially, The timber is hard and heavy, and has been used commercially to make furniture, pallets, and veneer.
